A bit about me...

Hi, I'm Dean Parker. I'm a blues function musician originally from Reading. Here's where my story began...

My Roots

I had my first guitar lesson when I was 9, and had them through my school for about a year. After getting bored, my mum encouraged me to continue and asked my uncle to teach me. He did so for 6 months but had to stop, but by this time I was improving, and good enough/enjoyed it enough to not want to stop this time.
I've been teaching myself ever since, both guitar and music theory, and have studied music for GCSE and A Level.

My Experience

I've played to live audiences of thousands, I have a bi-monthly gig at a local arts centre, and I'm a part of several local bands. I play the cajon regularly, and have dabbled in bass guitar too. I've played electric guitar to grade five standard, and learnt music theory to grade five too.
I've been learning and performing since the age of 9, and so with over a decade of musical experience there's not much I haven't seen. But, what I think is most important, is that my love for music has never ceased!

My Band

My band is a blues trio, with acoustic guitar, vocals, bass guitar and drums.
We sometimes include keys, too.

Me, Dean Parker

I lead the band on acoustic guitar and main vocals.
I also play electric, bass, cajon, ukelele and harmonica.

Dennis Armstrong

Dennis is my bassist and often sings backing vocals.
He's a musician who never ceases to amaze me - always improving, always showing me the latest skill or technique he's learnt - and I've worked with him for almost 3 years now.

Paul Youngs

Paul is my drummer.
He's the best musician I've ever played with - he's an unnaturally natural musician. He's taught himself other instruments too, and is one of the best pianists I know.
